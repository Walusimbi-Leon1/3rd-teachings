1st Class Teachings: The Beginning (Initiated: July 1, 2025)

1st Course: Introduction

1st Topic: Introduction

1.1.1.1. The teachings studied here are for receiving the Holy Spirit. God is the one who gives the Holy Spirit. The teachings here show you and make you fit and ready to receive the Holy Spirit. At the end of these teachings, you will realize that you have received the Holy Spirit without even knowing it.

1.1.1.2. Who is the Holy Spirit? - To those who are Christians, I know that this question is easy for you to answer. The Holy Spirit is simply the righteous Spirit of God that He gave to the world through Jesus Christ. - (Isaiah 11:2-3 EASY) [2] The Spirit of the Lord will be with him. The Spirit will make him wise, so that he understands things. The Spirit will show him what is right, and he will have the power to do it. The Spirit will cause him to know the Lord and to serve him truly. [3] To obey the Lord will make him very happy. He will not judge people because of what he sees with his eyes. He will not judge them because of what his ears hear about them.

1.1.1.3. What can the Holy Spirit do? - Since the Holy Spirit is God's Spirit, He can do everything except evil. - (Habakkuk 1:13 EASY) [13] But you are too holy to accept anything that is evil. You cannot agree with people who do wrong things. - The Bible has told us that God cannot accept anything that is evil. In the same way, God's Holy Spirit cannot accept to do anything evil.

1.1.1.4. How will the Holy Spirit help me? - This is a question that has no obvious answer, but you can think about this: - (Genesis 1:27 ERV) [27] So God created humans in his own image. He created them to be like himself. He created them male and female. - God created humans in His own image, but we know that God is a Spirit. - (John 4:24 ERV) [24] God is spirit. So the people who worship him must worship in spirit and truth. - This verse shows us two connected truths. "God is Spirit" shows us the nature of God. "…must worship him in spirit and truth" shows us that those who worship also have a spirit. - So, God created humans in His own image. But we know that God is a Spirit. This means that God created a spirit in humans. In a human body, there is a spirit that God created. Unfortunately, the spirit in the human body is weak and it can do nothing. When Satan (the spirit that God sent out of heaven) comes, he does everything he wishes to the human spirits because they are weak and can do nothing to protect themselves. - Now, this is what the Holy Spirit does. Satan cannot win over the Holy Spirit. - (Mark 16:17-18 ERV) [17] And the people who believe will be able to do these things as proof: They will use my name to force demons out of people. They will speak in languages they never learned. [18] If they pick up snakes or drink any poison, they will not be hurt. They will lay their hands on sick people, and they will get well. - After studying these lessons, you will be able to do more than what is written there. You will get dreams of what is to happen. You will get visions about various circumstances. You will be able to see into the lives of anyone who asks you a question. All kinds of sickness and diseases will obey you.

2nd Topic: Thoughts (Th)

  • God cannot be near an evil person, and 99% of your thoughts are evil. Only 1% of your thoughts are righteous. But to see God, you must be 100% righteous. God does not look at evil.
  • In this Topic, you are going to learn how to bring your thoughts into prison. You will destroy all evil thoughts out of your mind. This is to prepare a place where the Holy Spirit will stay in you. The Holy Spirit cannot be in you when you think the way you think right now.
  • Thoughts is a wide topic. A person can think more than 100 thoughts in just 1 minute.
  • If you need the Holy Spirit, you must change your thinking. How are you going to do this?
  • You will create a spreadsheet (Excel) file in the WPS Office or the word processing software that you use. In that file, include and write these words and highlight their boxes to show that they are headings.
  • Thoughts: In the column for thoughts, you must write every thought that you get in your life. Everything that you think of doing in your life, you must write it there.
  • (Ephesians 1:4) God chose us in Christ before the foundation of the world, that we should be holy and blameless before Him.
  • God's Spirit sanctifies you — sets you apart, makes you special — for closeness with God. The result is that you share in the glory of the risen Lord. This is not the earthly glory of the rich and famous. This is the glory of being with and like God's Son. He fills your life with purpose and joy.
  • God chose you as the firstfruits to be saved, through sanctification by the Spirit and belief in the truth. To this He called you through our Gospel, so that you may obtain the glory of our Lord Jesus Christ (2 Thessalonians 2:13-14).
  • Some words you will need a dictionary to understand what they really mean. Do not read and not understand. Use a dictionary to understand the meaning better.
  • You may download an English Dictionary on your phone, like the Vocapture app. But make sure you read and understand the meaning of every word.
  • God's Spirit is there for you from the beginning. Just as fleshly life came through your parents, spiritual birth comes from the Holy Spirit.
  • Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God. That which is born of the flesh is flesh, and that which is born of the Spirit is spirit (John 3:5-6).
  • Babies are not born alone. Babies are born to mothers who hold and feed them. Likewise, God gives us new life by His Spirit, "who He poured out on us richly" — covering us and caring for us.

1.1.2.3. Thoughts Practical Application: - As I said earlier, thoughts determine a lot concerning the Holy Spirit. You must be able to destroy all evil thoughts that come into your mind. To do this, you must start by writing every thought of anything that you think of doing. After you have written that thought, you write the date and time at which you got that thought. After that, you must wait 2 full days before doing that thing. That is the way of dealing with thoughts. Even if you clearly see that what you want to do is good, you should first give it 2 full days before doing it. - Not all things that we want to do are good. For example, in that photo on number 55, I wanted to change the plate that I use to eat food, but I did not know that what I wanted to do was wrong. But because I waited for some days to pass, like 7 days, I realized that what I wanted to do was wrong. That is why I placed an X on that thought. So this is how your life should be if you want to be free from the influence of Satan. - On number 56 in that photo, you can see that I wanted to start taking milk to a student at school. We all know that helping is good. But even if it is good, I first had to give it some time, which is 2 days. So this is how you should also do it. Whatever you think of doing, first write it somewhere and wait for 2 days to pass, then you can do it. - The purpose of the 2-day wait is to separate your own desires from the leading of the Holy Spirit. Many evil things come as sudden ideas. When you wait, you give your spirit time to examine the thought. You give the Holy Spirit time to show you if that thought is from God or from the enemy. Do not rush to act on any thought, no matter how good it seems. Write it, wait, and then decide.

3rd Course: Prayer and Gifts

1st Topic: Needs

1.3.1.1. (Philippians 4:6 ERV) [6] Don't worry about anything, but pray and ask God for everything you need, always giving thanks for what you have.

1.3.1.2. The Bible has instructed us not to worry about anything. "Don't worry about anything..." That is what the Bible has told us. We should not worry about anything, no matter how many problems we have. We should not worry.

1.3.1.3. "...but pray and ask God for everything you need..." The Bible has also told us to pray and ask God for everything we need. This is similar to what was written in 1 John 5:15.

1.3.1.4. (1 John 5:15 EASY) [15] Since we know that, we also know that God will give us the things that we ask for. It is as if we have already received those things from him.

1.3.1.5. The Bible has also told us "...we also know that God will give us the things that we ask for..." This should make us sure that whatever we ask for from God, we will receive it. Jesus Christ Himself said: - (John 14:13-14 EASY) [13] Ask me to help you. If you ask anything in my name, I will do it for you. In that way, the Son will show everyone how great the Father is. [14] Yes, you should ask me for anything in my name. I will do it for you.

1.3.1.6. Note what Jesus Himself said: "If you ask anything..." Now I want you to remember what Philippians 4:6 said: - (Philippians 4:6 ERV) [6] Don't worry about anything, but pray and ask God for everything you need, always giving thanks for what you have. - The word "anything" means all things. Now the Bible is telling us not to worry about anything, but we should pray and ask God for anything we need. And in John 14:13, Jesus tells us that "If you ask anything..." He said "anything." This means He meant all things. Whether your problem is poverty, sickness, problems, evil spirits — no matter what it is, He said, "...if you ask anything in my name, I will do it for you." Now, what are your problems? Is it poverty? Are you looking for a good career? Is it sickness? Demon attacks? Are you looking for a job? Are bad dreams disturbing you? What is it that is bothering you? What is hindering your progress? What is hindering your success?

1.3.1.7. Now you should not be worried anymore. Jesus Christ has told us He will do anything that we ask for (John 14:13). - But in doing this, we must not forget what God said. Listen to this: - (Psalms 27:14 EASY) [14] Wait for the Lord to help you! Be strong and brave. Yes, wait for the Lord to help you! - The Bible has told us, "...wait for the Lord to help you!" We should be patient as we wait for God's help. We should not say, "God is not going to help me. He did not hear my prayer." No, that is wrong. God hears what we say in prayer. Listen to this: - (1 Peter 3:12 EASY) [12] Remember that the Lord God sees what everyone does. He takes care of people who do what is right. When they pray, he listens to them. But the Lord God turns against people who do evil things.

The Bible is telling us, when people who do what is right pray, God listens to them. So we are sure that our prayers will be answered. All we need to do is to trust God and have patience.

1.3.1.8. (Psalms 37:7 EASY) [7] Be patient as you wait for the Lord to help you. Trust him to bring help. Do not become upset when bad people seem to do well, when their wicked ideas really happen.

The Bible is telling us in (Psalms 37:7), "...wait for the Lord to help you!" To wait means that we should be patient as we wait for God's help. Some people turn away from God at this point because they do not understand what God's word says. Listen again to what God's Spirit said through David: - (Psalms 37:7 EASY) [7] Be patient as you wait for the Lord to help you. Trust him to bring help. Do not become upset when bad people seem to do well, when their wicked ideas really happen. - Also David himself said in Psalms 40:1. Listen to what he said: - (Psalms 40:1 EASY) [1] I was patient while I waited for the Lord to help me. He turned towards me, and he heard me when I called to him. David talks about patience as part of faith. Some people give up not because they do not trust in God, but because they have no patience to wait for God. God wants to see if you really trust in Him. He wants to see you wait for Him. Listen to what the Bible says in James 1:3: - (James 1:3 EASY) [3] God wants to see if you really trust him. Your faith in God will become stronger as a result of these troubles. God only wants to see that your trust in Him is true. That is what James 1:3 is telling us. So you should be ready to face any circumstances. You should not give up. You should always show that your trust in God is true. Then God will do whatever you ask.

1.3.1.9. What should I do before asking God for what I want? - The answer to this question is in Mark 11:25. Listen to what Jesus Christ said: - (Mark 11:25 EASY) [25] But when you stand up to pray, first you must forgive other people. If anyone has done something bad against you, forgive that person. If you do forgive them, your Father in heaven will forgive you. God will forgive you for the wrong things that you have done.

Jesus has told us, "first you must forgive other people..." When you do not forgive other people, God will also not forgive us for the wrong things we have done. And if God has not forgiven us, He can do nothing to help us. Someone might say, "But for me, no person has done wrong to me today. I have not met any person who has done wrong to me today." If it is like that, still that person has to forgive the people who did wrong things to him or her some time back. Another person might have anger towards someone. Even if that anger has been on his or her heart for many years, that person should still forgive the people who did wrong to them. Another thing might be when you do not like someone. That is all sin before God. So you should first forgive other people, then God will give you everything that you ask for. - Whether someone did something wrong to you or not, you should forgive those people. This is how you should begin your prayer: - "My Loving Father in Heaven, I confess that my life is full of sin. Loving Father in Heaven, I forgive everyone who has done wrong to me. And everyone who did wrong to me, I forgive them all. Loving Father in Heaven, I thank you for your everlasting Love..." - Then you can add to your prayer from that beginning. You can ask God for anything you want Him to do for you. - That beginning part of the prayer carries the first basic parts of faith in God. First, you confess your sins. By this, we show God that we are sinners and we are not worthy to receive His help. Listen to what the Bible says in Proverbs 28:13:

1.3.1.10. (Proverbs 28:13 EASY) [13] Anyone who hides his sins will never do well. But God will forgive somebody if he agrees that he has done wrong things, and if he turns away from his sins.

"...But God will forgive somebody, if he agrees that he has done wrong things..." Yes, God will forgive us when we confess our sins in prayer. This is what John also wrote about in 1 John 1:9: - (1 John 1:9 EASY) [9] Instead, we should agree that we have done wrong things and tell that to God. Then God will forgive us for our sins. He always does what he has promised to do. He always does what is right. He will forgive us and he will make us clean again, whatever bad things we may have done. When we pray, we should also confess our sins. God will forgive us if we accept that we are sinners. When we ask God for something, we should find somewhere to write what we have asked. Then when God answers our prayers, we should mark or tick that prayer request. When we do this, our faith in God will grow because we will realize how and when God answers our prayers. This is what is named "Needs." We find somewhere to write all that we request from God. Then when those things are fulfilled, we mark or tick our prayer requests.

1.3.1.11. When we ask God for something, we should make sure that we write everything that we ask. This helps in making sure whether you get everything you ask for or you don't. I also did not know this trick. But in the month of May 2025, I asked God for some things. I remember I asked for joy, peace, patience, and things like that. On the first day, I did not receive any of those things, but I continued to ask. Remember, I had written those things in my spreadsheet, so I could check on the things I asked God for every morning. This topic is called Needs because that is the name I gave to the things I asked for from God. So I could ask God for these needs every day. Then after some time, like 15 days, I realized that I had received the things that I had asked for. Then I continued writing every need that I needed. Everything that I thought that I needed, I started asking for it from God, and I received most of the things that I asked for. This made me prove the existence of God. - Now this is what the Needs topic is all about. Do not just ask for things from God, but first write whatever you need, then you can ask God for those things. - If you want to start now with the Needs, you can create a spreadsheet and call it Needs. Then you write there all the things that you ask God to do for you. Or you can get a book and write there all your needs, but that will be tiresome and it is old-fashioned. Start now with the "Needs" knowledge you have got, and see how God is going to change your life.

2nd Topic: Abilities

1.3.2.1. A person can carry out more than 10 tests a day without noticing. Plus, a person can do tests that are not of his own, but tests from other people. My children, my aim is for you to receive the Holy Spirit. So I will speak the true facts of what you must know. Then after you have received the Holy Spirit, you will understand the love that I have for you. This is true love, that I give you the right teachings, intended for receiving the Holy Spirit, as the Scriptures say: - (1 John 3:24 EASY) [24] Anyone who obeys God's commands lives together with God. God also lives in him. How do we know that God lives in us? It is because of his Holy Spirit that he has given to us.

So my intentions are Holy, and they align with God's will. But the people of this world hated God and His Son Jesus Christ. People use their mouths to show that they honor God, but that is all a lie. So God looks at them and He laughs. He sees deep inside their thoughts and hearts and He knows that they are lying when they say they honor Him. Listen to what David told his son Solomon: - (1 Chronicles 28:9 ERV) [9] "And you, my son Solomon, know the God of your father. Serve God with a pure heart. Be happy to serve him, because the Lord knows what is in everyone's heart. He knows what you are thinking. If you go to him for help, you will get an answer. But if you turn away from him, he will leave you forever."

1.3.2.2. Abilities are special things that a certain person can do when other people cannot do those things. Abilities are in the spirit, not in the body, because all people have similar bodies but different spirits. For example, Jesus had an ability of healing people. An ability is something that makes you unique in a specific field or society of activity. For example, if you are a teacher, an ability is something that makes you different from other teachers. It is that thing that you know you have and you use it effectively and other teachers do not have it. Instead, other teachers just say, "How do you do it?" You have heard people saying, "How did he do it?" It is the ability in his or her spirit that makes them do what other people think is impossible to do.

1.3.2.3. For example, one of my abilities is waking up at 4:00 AM every day, thinking about Bible Verses, and many more. These abilities make me unique from a crowd, and they help me in knowing God more. Abilities save days, they make life easy, and they help one live a steady life just like how the Daily Schedule does it. There are a lot of Abilities, and these are determined by factors like age, gender, society, relationship status (married and single), financial status (rich or poor). But remember: no rich person will ever enter the kingdom of God state of mind. All these determine the Abilities that a particular person can have. Now, to get started, think of Abilities that can solve particular problems in your day. Abilities are in the spirit, so it is God's Spirit in us which makes us able to have those Abilities. You personally can do nothing, but God's Spirit in you will make you fit for every ability that you wish. When you need something, you run after it. In the same way, you must run for Abilities if you really need them. You must be strong, though they sometimes look difficult at first. But soon you will get familiar with them.

1.3.2.4. Here is how you start. You create a spreadsheet, name it Abilities. The sheets are the Abilities. Every sheet must have its own ability. Or, if you are traditional, you can write your Abilities in a book. Every ability must have its own page. Remember to pray to God for these Abilities. In the "Needs" file, you must write things like these, asking God to make you able for every ability that you want.

4th Course: Humans

1st Topic: Tests

1.4.1.1. Since we are all humans, we all face similar troubles and challenges. Even though some of these challenges are determined by various factors, we all count God as the cause. I laugh at people who lament and cry because of their problems. Yes, their problems are true, and the problems really exist. But the reason why I laugh is because people themselves cause those problems. I tried to put it on God Himself at first, but it was a great lie and a complex equation to solve. Therefore, I declared two things: "Personal Tests" and "People Tests." Here is where we get the name "Tests." If you carefully look at everything done by humans, they are all tests. God Himself tested when He created the earth. The earth was not there, so God took a broad test and He created the earth, not knowing what would come as a result. So it is the same with us humans. But our testing as humans is so severe that a person can carry out more than 100 Tests in a single day. That is like saving 100 weapons to attack God. No one can leave testing, but we can all limit testing. If we limit testing, we limit the challenges and problems in our lives.

1.4.1.2. Since all things exist by "testing," we cannot deny testing. But testing should not be a burden. It should not be a sin. It should be the will of God. The Abilities we receive all come through testing. But also sin and bad acts come through testing. When you test sinning for the first time, you are more likely to do that sin again. So this Topic totally restricts us from testing bad things. And in most things, some things are bad but we do not know that they are bad.

1.4.1.3. Just like all other Topics, you need to write down the tests that you do every day. All this comes from the Daily Schedule. When you do not follow your Daily Schedule very well, you might find yourself doing other things which you never even included in your "Thoughts file." Tests that should be done should be those written first in the Thoughts file. If not, those tests are sinful passions that rise from our true nature.

1.4.1.4. The "People Tests" are those tests caused by people. These tests do not come from a person's spirit, but they come from the people. So these tests can be restricted by avoiding meeting people, not even hearing from people. They can only be controlled by keeping yourself away from people. Some of these People Tests include: People's Opinions, People's Advice, People's Instructions, and others. All this is considered nonsense because a person with the Holy Spirit must be above reproach. No one must advise them, instruct them, or give them an opinion. No one can advise a god. So everyone with the Holy Spirit is a god, and no one can advise that person.

1.4.1.5. "Personal Tests" are tests that a person's spirit starts by itself. They are not easy to control. They are like habits. They can cause depression, anxiety, and every emotion that feels worst.

1.4.1.6. So start now with the "Tests" knowledge. Write every test that bothers you, anything that may seem new to you, any disturbances. Things that you start yourself are called "Personal Tests." Then tests from people are called "People Tests."

2nd Topic: Days

1.4.2.1. Time is something greater than all things. Without time, nothing exists. We have considered time in almost every file that we have looked at, but I further defined it into its separate file called "Days." 2.7.1.6. The Holy Spirit worksheet is the most important worksheet in the Life File. In this worksheet, you track your relationship with the Holy Spirit Himself. This is not about formulas or calculations. This is about your personal walk with God. Here is how to set it up:

In the Holy Spirit worksheet, create these columns: - Column A — Date: The date of the entry. - Column B — Holy Spirit Experience: Write down any experience you had with the Holy Spirit that day. Did you feel His presence? Did He speak to you? Did He guide you in a decision? - Column C — Scripture: What Bible verse did the Holy Spirit use to speak to you today? - Column D — Obedience: Did you obey what the Holy Spirit told you to do? Write Yes or No and explain. - Column E — Growth: How did you grow closer to the Holy Spirit today? Write one sentence.

The formula for the Holy Spirit is: HS = DO / TO, where HS means Holy Spirit, DO means Days of Obedience (days when you obeyed the Holy Spirit's leading), and TO means Total Days in the period you are measuring. For example, if in 30 days you obeyed the Holy Spirit 22 times, then HS = 22/30 = 0.733 HS.

Remember: the Holy Spirit is a person, not a force. You can grieve Him (Ephesians 4:30). You can quench Him (1 Thessalonians 5:19). You can be filled with Him (Ephesians 5:18). The goal of all these Thirteen Files is to prepare you to be a dwelling place for the Holy Spirit. Without the Holy Spirit, all these files are just empty spreadsheets. But with the Holy Spirit, they become the very breath of God in your life.

2.7.1.7. Now let us return to the References worksheet. In References, we collect all the values from Sin, Fruits, Gifts, and Holy Spirit worksheets and we calculate the overall Life value. The formula for Life is: Li = (Ri + Fr + Gi + HS) / 4, where Li means Life, Ri means Righteousness (which is 1 - Sin), Fr means Fruits, Gi means Gifts, and HS means Holy Spirit.

For example, if on a specific day: - Ri = 0.375 (from Sin of 0.625) - Fr = 0.589 - Gi = 0.333 - HS = 0.733

Then Li = (0.375 + 0.589 + 0.333 + 0.733) / 4 = 2.030 / 4 = 0.508 Li. The units of Life are Li.

A Life value above 0.700 means you are living a Spirit-filled life. A Life value below 0.400 means you need to go back and work on your files. Remember: Life is the overall file. It represents YOU. So make sure your Life value is always growing, never decreasing.

Summary of All Thirteen Files

3.1.1. We have now come to the end of this book. But the end of this book is not the end of your journey. It is the beginning. Let us take a moment to remember what we have learned and how each of the Thirteen Files works together to build your spiritual life.

The Thirteen Files are:

  1. Daily Schedule (DS) — The foundation. It orders your day and makes your life predictable and holy. A person without a Daily Schedule is like a house without a foundation.

  2. Thoughts (Th) — The gateway. It controls what enters your mind and gives you time to examine every thought before you act on it. A person who controls their thoughts controls their life.

  3. Needs (ND) — The prayer list. It keeps track of everything you ask God for, so you can see when He answers. This builds your faith and proves the existence of God.

  4. Abilities (Ab) — The power. It develops the unique skills and gifts that God has placed in your spirit. Your abilities make you different from everyone else.

  5. Tests (Te) — The mirror. It shows you your bad habits and the tests that come from people. By tracking tests, you reduce sin in your life.

  6. Days (Da) — The calendar. It marks special days and helps you celebrate what God has done in your life. It also tracks fasting and everyday events.

  7. Goals (Go) — The engine. It breaks negative emotions and gives you something meaningful to work on. Goals make life exciting.

  8. Court (Co) — The judge. It creates laws that govern your life and punishes you when you break them. Without the Court, you have no discipline.

  9. Personal Duties (PD) — The body care. It makes sure you do what you must do to maintain your health and your environment.

  10. Accounts (Ac) — The wallet. It tracks every coin that comes in and goes out. Money is spiritual, and how you handle it affects your spirit.

  11. Accounts Scale of Preferences (ASP) — The desires. It lists everything you want to buy and tracks your wealth. Your desires show what is in your heart.

  12. Life (Li) — The summary. It brings together Sin, Fruits, Gifts, and the Holy Spirit into one overall value. Life is who you are.

  13. Spiritual Teachings (ST) — The wisdom. It records what the Holy Spirit teaches you personally. These teachings are unique to you.
